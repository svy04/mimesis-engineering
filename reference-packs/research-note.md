# Reference Pack: Research Note

Use this when a research note sounds smart but does not yet show source quality, method limits, or proof boundaries.

## Source Quality

Prefer papers, standards, patents, official docs, maintained source repos, datasets, and directly inspectable benchmark setups.
Use secondary commentary only to route back to the primary source.

## What Good Research Notes Usually Do

- state the problem precisely
- separate primary sources from secondary commentary
- explain why each source matters
- compare methods or claims
- show what is known, uncertain, and untested
- mark limitations and next experiments

## What To Inspect

- source type
- claim hierarchy
- method comparison
- evidence chain
- limitation section
- next-test design

## Transferable Patterns

- source-first evidence table
- claim-to-evidence map
- known / unknown split
- method comparison grid
- limitation-first conclusion
- next experiment section

## Do Not Copy

- abstracts or paper prose
- author authority without context
- benchmark numbers without source and setup
- conclusions beyond the cited evidence
- legal, medical, financial, or safety claims without qualified review

## Starter Prompt

```text
Study these primary sources and maintained implementations as standards.

Identify the problem statement, source quality, claim hierarchy, method comparison, evidence chain, limitations, and next experiment.

Then transform my research note under my actual evidence boundary.
Do not turn weak evidence into a strong claim.
```
