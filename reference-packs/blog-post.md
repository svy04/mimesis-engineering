# Blog Post Reference Pack

## Use When

Use this when a draft has an idea but no strong reading path.

## Source Quality

Prefer original essays, first-party engineering posts, papers, standards, source repos, and public talks with stable links.
Avoid derivative summaries when the original source is available.

## What Good Artifacts Usually Do

- state a thesis
- create tension
- move through a clear arc
- use examples
- keep insight density high
- avoid filler
- end with a useful next action

## What to Inspect

- thesis
- narrative arc
- tension
- examples
- insight density
- ending CTA

## Transferable Patterns

- problem-you-felt opening
- old belief vs new frame
- concrete example ladder
- compressed definitions
- proof or limitation section
- useful ending

## Do Not Copy

- exact metaphors
- personal stories
- sentence rhythm too closely
- named examples without context
- claims that belong to the reference author

## Starter Prompt

```text
Study the reference posts below.
Extract their structure, not their voice.

Identify thesis, tension, narrative arc, examples, insight density, proof boundaries, and ending CTA.

Then transform my draft while preserving my own evidence and constraints.
```
