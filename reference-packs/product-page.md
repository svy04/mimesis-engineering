# Product Page Reference Pack

## Use When

Use this when a product page explains features but does not make the use case feel concrete.

## Source Quality

Prefer official product pages, maintained docs, public demos, release notes, source repos, and help-center flows.
Avoid copying customer logos, pricing claims, or product screenshots you do not have permission to use.

## What Good Artifacts Usually Do

- name the audience
- anchor the use case
- show the workflow
- compare old and new behavior
- show trust devices
- surface proof
- make activation easy

## What to Inspect

- audience
- use case
- workflow
- proof
- comparison
- trust devices
- activation

## Transferable Patterns

- role-to-use-case shift
- workflow diagram
- first action CTA
- feature-to-outcome mapping
- proof boundary note
- limitation callout

## Do Not Copy

- exact feature names
- product screenshots
- customer logos
- testimonials
- benchmark claims
- pricing structure without context

## Starter Prompt

```text
Study the reference product pages below.
Extract their structure, not their claims.

Identify the audience, use case, workflow, proof rhythm, comparison frame, trust devices, and activation path.

Then transform my product page under my actual product, audience, evidence, and constraints.
```
