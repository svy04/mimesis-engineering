# Reference Pack: Profile Positioning

Use this when a GitHub profile, LinkedIn profile, personal homepage, or creator bio does not yet route attention to the right proof surface.

This pack is informed by the neighboring `mimesis-casebook` profile cases.

## Source Quality

Prefer local artifacts, public profiles, official project pages, source repos, case notes, and proof pages with visible claim boundaries.
Avoid private analytics, copied bios, and unverifiable social proof.

## What Good Profile Positioning Usually Does

- states the current thesis quickly
- routes attention to the strongest proof surface
- names the work without pretending maturity
- links to the method, case, or offer
- removes stale identity claims
- gives one next action

## What To Inspect

- first-line thesis
- project/repo order
- proof links
- offer or collaboration path
- claim boundary
- stale claims
- social proof risk

## Transferable Patterns

- thesis first
- proof surface second
- repo or case links third
- boundary statement near claims
- one action path
- distribution surface separate from source of truth

## Do Not Copy

- another person's title
- authority signals you have not earned
- employer or affiliation language
- unverifiable outcome claims
- fake community proof
- copied post structure

## Starter Prompt

```text
Study these profile and positioning artifacts as standards.

Identify the thesis, proof surface, routing path, stale claims, and claim boundary.

Then transform my profile under my actual proof and audience constraints.
Do not invent external validation.
```
