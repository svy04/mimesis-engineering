# Landing Page Reference Pack

## Use When

Use this when a landing page feels generic, unclear, overdesigned, or unearned.

## Source Quality

Prefer official product pages, official docs, docs-linked pages, public changelogs, pricing pages, and case pages with visible proof boundaries.
Avoid swipe files, screenshots without permission, and summaries that hide the original page.

## What Good Artifacts Usually Do

- make the hero clear
- name the pain quickly
- show the transformation promise
- give one obvious CTA
- show proof or limits
- handle objections
- respect the claim boundary

## What to Inspect

- hero clarity
- pain statement
- transformation promise
- proof
- CTA
- objection handling
- boundary

## Transferable Patterns

- one-line offer
- before/after contrast
- proof before pressure
- short objection blocks
- visible boundary language
- action-first page flow

## Do Not Copy

- exact hero wording
- brand voice
- visual identity
- screenshots you do not own
- customer proof you do not have
- pricing claims without proof

## Starter Prompt

```text
Study the reference landing pages below.
Extract structure, not surface.

Identify their hero clarity, pain statement, transformation promise, proof rhythm, CTA path, objection handling, and claim boundary.

Then transform my landing page under my audience, proof, and product constraints.
Do not invent customer proof.
```
